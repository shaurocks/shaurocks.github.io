  • What is health ?
    1. “Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity.”

    2. In 1986, the WHO made further clarifications: “Health is a resource for everyday life, not the objective of living.



  • What is the W.H.O. ?
    1. The world health organization works worldwide to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable.

    2. Their goal is to ensure that a billion more people have universal health coverage, to protect a billion more people from health emergencies, and provide a further billion people with better health and well-being.


  • How does pollution affect our health ?
    1. Both short- and long-term exposure to air pollution can lead to a wide range of diseases.

    2. When we breathe in air pollutants, they can enter our bloodstream and contribute to coughing or itchy eyes and cause or worsen many breathing and lung diseases, leading to hospitalizations, cancer, or even premature death.



  • How can we make our lungs strong ?
    1. Stop Smoking (and avoid secondhand smoke). If you currently use tobacco products, quitting smoking is the fastest way to improve lung health.

    2. Exercise regularly. When you're physically active, your heart and lungs work harder to get additional oxygen to your muscles.

    3. Maintain a healthy diet and stay hydrated.

    4. Get annual check-ups. Visiting your healthcare provider regularly for a check-up helps prevent illness.
